# Audience & Context Analysis

## Programme Details

- **Institution**: University of Greenwich, Business School, London
- **Programme**: MSc Data Analytics & Management
- **Cohort Size**: ~200 students
- **Background Mix**:
  - 67% Business background
  - 15% Computer Science
  - 18% Statistics / Mathematics

## Prior Knowledge (by lecture date)

Students will have covered:

- Machine learning basics
- Basic prediction models
- The concept of agency (theoretical)

## Constraints

- **No hands-on coding** during the lecture
- **Microsoft Copilot** is the only AI tool permitted under current licensing
- Lecture should **bridge theory to practice** without requiring participants to write code

## Key Focus Areas (from university brief)

1. Real-world implementations of agentic AI
2. Practical applications in business contexts
3. Governance considerations

## Audience Implications

| Segment | Implication |
|---------|-------------|
| Business majority (67%) | Lead with business value, ROI, use cases; avoid jargon-heavy technical depth |
| CS minority (15%) | Provide architectural depth as enrichment, not prerequisite |
| Stats/Maths (18%) | Leverage comfort with models, metrics, and data-driven reasoning |
| No coding allowed | Demos must be visual/interactive; use Copilot as the relatable entry point |

## Pedagogical Approach

- **Conceptual scaffolding**: Build from their ML/agency foundations toward agentic AI
- **Visual demonstrations**: Show, don't tell - live demos of agent systems in action
- **Business case framing**: Every technical concept tied to a business outcome
- **Governance woven in**: Not an afterthought - integrated throughout as responsible AI practice
