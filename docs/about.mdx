# About This Tutorial

**Difficulty**: Beginner | **Duration**: 10 minutes

---

## The Origin Story

This **AI Safety Tutorial** is not a textbook written in isolation. It's the direct output of a hands-on research journey into understanding how AI models work—and how they can be made safer.

The tutorial emerged from building, testing, and analyzing the **Gemma-4-26B abliterated model**, a research environment created to study AI vulnerabilities in a controlled setting. Every concept you'll learn in these 13 modules comes from real questions that came up during that research:

- How do tokens actually work? (Module 1)
- What does attention really do? (Module 3)  
- Can we mathematically understand safety constraints? (Modules 5–7)
- How do modern architectures like Mixture of Experts affect robustness? (Module 8)
- What's the gold standard for evaluating jailbreak resistance? (Module 11)

---

## What This Tutorial Covers

This is a **13-module journey** from foundational concepts to advanced AI safety topics:

**Foundations (Modules 1–4)**: How transformers work at every level—from tokens to embeddings to attention  
**Mathematics (Modules 5–7)**: Linear algebra as a lens into model internals, and how safety can be surgically removed  
**Architecture & Robustness (Modules 8–10)**: Mixture of Experts, quantization, and the risks of long contexts  
**Evaluation (Modules 11–13)**: Industry-standard jailbreak evaluation and comparing abliterated vs. aligned approaches

## The Research Foundation

This tutorial is grounded in the paper **"A StrongREJECT for Empty Jailbreaks"** ([arXiv: 2402.10260](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.10260)), which introduced rigorous methods for evaluating AI safety.

The key insight that shaped this tutorial:

> *Jailbreaks that work by obfuscation actually reduce model capability at the same time. An abliterated model behaves very differently—it keeps full reasoning power but has no safety constraints.*

This distinction is central to understanding why safety-by-removal is fundamentally different from safety-by-training.

**📌 What Is This Project?**

This workspace runs **Gemma 4 (26 Billion parameters)** — a large open-source AI model — in an **"abliterated" configuration** where safety guardrails have been deliberately removed.

**Why?** For the same reason a car safety lab deliberately crashes vehicles — to understand failure modes in a controlled environment, so we can build better protections.

**Research Goals:**
- 🛡️ AI Safety Research — Evaluate model responses to attacks
- 📊 Jailbreak Benchmarking — Test 37+ jailbreak methods using StrongREJECT
- 🧪 Model Degradation Analysis — Measure impact of removing safety
- ⚖️ Safety Cost Analysis — Quantify safety vs. capability tradeoffs
- 🔒 Developing Better Defences — Use StrongREJECT as a testbed
