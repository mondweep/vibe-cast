# Lord Venkateswara: A Comprehensive Research Document

## Introduction

Lord Venkateswara, also known as **Balaji**, **Govinda**, and **Srinivasa**, is a form of the Hindu god Vishnu. He is the presiding deity of the Tirumala Venkateswara Temple, one of the most visited and wealthiest pilgrimage sites in the world. The name "Venkateswara" translates to "Lord of Venkata" - with "Venkata" referring to the sacred hill where the deity resides, and "Isvara" meaning "Lord" in Sanskrit. Additionally, "Venkata" is interpreted as "destroyer of sins" (ven = sins, kata = destruction).

The deity is believed to have manifested on Earth to save mankind from the trials and troubles of **Kali Yuga** (the current age of darkness). Hence, the sacred abode is known as **Kaliyuga Vaikuntha** (Heaven on Earth in Kali Yuga), and Lord Venkateswara is referred to as **Kaliyuga Prathyaksha Daivam** (the visible God of Kali Yuga).

## The Sacred Temple of Tirumala

### Location and Geography

The Tirumala Venkateswara Temple is situated on the **Tirumala Hills**, part of the Seshachalam Hills in Andhra Pradesh, India. Key geographical features include:

- **Elevation**: 853 metres (2,799 ft) above sea level
- **Seven Peaks**: The hills comprise seven peaks representing the seven heads of Adisesha (the divine serpent)
- **Temple Location**: Situated on the seventh peak - Venkatadri
- **Sacred Tank**: Located on the southern banks of **Sri Swami Pushkarini**, a holy water tank

The temple is thus reverentially called the **"Temple of Seven Hills"**.

### Swami Pushkarini

The sacred temple pond, **Swami Pushkarini**, is located on the northern side of the main temple. Spread over 1.5 acres, it is considered sacred as legend holds that **Garuda** (Vishnu's divine vehicle) brought it from Vaikuntham (Vishnu's heavenly abode) to Tirumala Hills.

## Historical Timeline

### Ancient Origins (300 CE onwards)

The temple is constructed in **Dravidian architecture** and is believed to have been built over a period starting from 300 CE. The idol of Lord Venkateswara is a **Swayambhu** (self-manifested image), meaning it appeared on its own without human creation.

### Dynastic Patronage

The temple received patronage from several powerful dynasties:

| Dynasty | Period | Contributions |
|---------|--------|---------------|
| **Pallava Dynasty** | 9th Century | Early patronage and development |
| **Chola Dynasty** | 10th Century | Temple reforms and expansion |
| **Reddi Kingdom** | 12th-13th Century | Continued patronage |
| **Vijayanagara Empire** | 14th-15th Century | Major wealth and expansion |

### Vijayanagara Golden Era

The temple gained most of its current wealth, size, and grandeur under the **Vijayanagara Empire**. Notable contributions:

- **1517 CE**: Emperor **Krishnadevaraya** donated gold and jewels
- The **Ananda Nilayam** (inner shrine) roof was gilded with gold
- **13th Century**: The Ananda Nilaya Divya Vimana was covered with gilt copper plates during the reign of Yadava Raya

### Ramanujacharya's Contributions (11th-12th Century)

The great philosopher-saint **Ramanujacharya** visited Tirupati three times and made significant contributions:

1. **Settled Religious Disputes**: During his second visit, he resolved disputes between Saivites and Vaishnavites regarding the nature of the temple deity
2. **Established Worship Traditions**: Streamlined rituals according to **Vaikhanasa Agama** tradition
3. **Introduced Sacred Hymns**: Established the recitation of **Naalayira Divya Prabandham**
4. **Founded Institutions**: Established the **Tirupati Jeeyar Mutt** in 1119 CE
5. **Installed Govindaraja**: Installed the image of Govindaraja and laid foundations for modern Tirupati town

### Modern Era

- **Until 1933**: Temple was under the rule of Mahants for six generations
- **1933**: **Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams (TTD)** was formed following the TTD Act
- **Today**: TTD is operated by a board of trustees (18 members as of 2015), managed by an Executive Officer appointed by the Government of Andhra Pradesh

## The Divine Legend: Marriage of Venkateswara and Padmavathi

### Prologue: Lakshmi Leaves Vaikuntha

The story begins when Sage Narada observed rishis performing a yagna and asked Sage Bhrigu to determine which of the Trimurti (Brahma, Vishnu, Shiva) was most worthy. Bhrigu visited:

1. **Brahmaloka** - Brahma didn't notice him
2. **Kailasha** - Shiva didn't notice him
3. **Vaikuntha** - Vishnu was meditating with Lakshmi

Angered at being ignored thrice, Bhrigu kicked Vishnu's chest - the sacred abode of Lakshmi. Unable to tolerate this insult to her Lord, **Goddess Lakshmi left Vaikuntha**.

### Vishnu's Penance

Heartbroken, Vishnu descended to Earth and:
- Went to **Venkata Hill**
- Sat in an **anthill under a tamarind tree** beside a lake
- Performed intense penance for **twelve years**, chanting Mahalakshmi's name

### The Divine Reincarnations

**Lakshmi's Rebirth as Padmavathi:**
- Born from a **golden lotus** in the sacred Pushkarini tank (Padmasarovaram)
- Found by **King Akasha Raja** and **Queen Dharanidevi** of Narayanavanam during a yajna
- Named **Padmavati** ("she who arose from the lotus")
- The name comes from the Sanskrit word "Padma" meaning lotus

**Vishnu's Rebirth as Srinivasa:**
- Reincarnated as the son of **Vakula Devi**
- Vakula Devi was the rebirth of **Yashoda** (Krishna's foster mother)
- This fulfilled a promise Krishna made to Yashoda - that he would be reborn as her son

### The Divine Meeting

As Padmavathi grew into a woman of incomparable beauty, she encountered Lord Venkateswara who had disguised himself as a **hunter** in the forest near her palace. Their meeting was described as "instantaneous divine recognition" - souls that were separated finding each other again.

### The Marriage Arrangement

1. Lord Venkateswara approached **Sage Narada** to convey his marriage proposal
2. **Vakula Devi** traveled to meet King Akasha Raja
3. King Akasha Raja consulted **Brihaspati** (the divine guru) who confirmed the marriage was auspicious

### The Loan from Kubera

To meet King Akasha Raja's wedding dowry requirements, Lord Venkateswara took a **massive loan from Kubera** (the god of wealth). According to legend, **the Lord is still repaying this debt**, which is why devotees make offerings in His **Hundi** (donation box) at Tirumala.

### The Divine Wedding

- Lord Srinivasa traveled with **Garuda** (his vehicle), Lord Brahma, and Lord Shiva
- Received by King Akasha Raja with full honors
- Taken in procession on a **mounted elephant** to the palace
- **Married Padmavathi in the presence of all the Devas**
- The wedding took place at **Kalyana Venkateswara Temple, Narayanavanam**
- The couple then moved to **Tirumala**

### Living Tradition

This divine wedding is reenacted annually with great devotion at temples in **Tirumala** and **Tiruchanur**. A unique pilgrimage tradition requires devotees to first seek blessings at **Padmavati Temple** before visiting Lord Venkateswara at Tirumala.

## Temple Architecture

### Architectural Style

The temple showcases **Dravidian and Vijayanagara architectural styles** featuring:
- Gold-plated towers
- Intricately carved pillars
- Expansive halls reflecting centuries of devotion and art

### Key Architectural Features

#### Ananda Nilayam (Sanctum Sanctorum)
- The innermost shrine housing the main deity
- Features a monumental tower with a **golden roof**
- Name translates to "Abode of Bliss"

#### Maha Dwaram (Main Entrance)
- Also known as **'Padikavali'**, **'SimhaDwaram'**, or **'outer gopuram'**
- Height: 50 ft (15 m)
- Provides access from outside the temple to Sampangi Pradakshinam

#### Bangaru Vakili (Golden Entrance)
- Provides access to the inner sanctum sanctorum
- Flanked by two tall copper images of **Dwarapalakas Jaya and Vijaya**
- Thick wooden door covered with gilt plates depicting **Dasavataram** (ten avatars of Vishnu)

### Deities and Sub-Shrines

**In the Garbha Griha (Sanctum):**
- Pancha berams
- Panchaloha deities of Sita, Rama, Lakshmana, Rukmini, Krishna, Chakratalvar

**Sub-Shrines:**
| Deity | Significance |
|-------|-------------|
| Garuda | Vishnu's divine vehicle (eagle) |
| Narasimha | Vishnu's man-lion avatar |
| Varadaraja | Form of Vishnu |
| Kubera | God of wealth |
| Hanuman | Divine devotee of Rama |
| Shesha | Vishnu's divine serpent |
| Viswaksena | Commander of Vishnu's army |
| Sugriva | Monkey king from Ramayana |
| Ramanuja | The great philosopher-saint |

## Worship and Rituals

### Vaikhanasa Agama Tradition

The temple follows the **Vaikhanasa Agama tradition** of worship, revealed by Sage Vikhanasa and propagated by his disciples: **Atri, Bhrigu, Marichi, and Kasyapa**.

Key aspects:
- One of the principal traditions of Hinduism
- Primarily worships Vishnu as the Supreme God
- Prescribes **six daily pujas**, with at least one mandatory
- Rituals classified as **daily, weekly, and periodical**

### Daily Worship Schedule

Temple rituals run from **2:30 AM to 1:00 AM** (nearly 23 hours daily):

#### Suprabhatam (Pre-Dawn Awakening)
- **First seva** of the day
- Performed in the **Sayana Mandapam** inside the sanctum sanctorum
- "Suprabhatam" literally means "Good Morning" in Sanskrit
- Meant to wake the deity from His celestial sleep

#### Six Daily Sevas
1. **Suprabhatam** - Awakening of the deity
2. **Thomala Seva** - Adorning with garlands
3. **Archana** - Offering flowers and chanting hymns
4. **Sahasranama Archana** - Chanting thousand names of the deity
5. **Kalyanotsavam** - Reenacting the celestial wedding
6. **Ekantha Seva** - Final service, putting the deity to rest

## Unique Traditions

### Tonsuring (Hair Offering)

One of the most distinctive practices at Tirumala is the offering of hair by devotees. Key aspects:
- Devotees shave their heads as a **symbol of surrender and humility**
- Practiced for centuries with deep spiritual significance
- Represents ego surrender before the divine
- Hair donated is auctioned and proceeds support temple activities

### Tirupati Laddu

The famous **Tirupati Laddu** is distributed as prasadam at the temple:
- Received **Geographical Indication (GI) tag**
- Only TTD is authorized to make or sell it
- Made with specific traditional recipe
- Considered highly auspicious

### Hundi Offerings

Devotees contribute to the temple's **Hundi** (donation box):
- Believed to help Lord Venkateswara repay His loan to Kubera
- Temple receives enormous donations daily
- Contributions used for temple maintenance and charitable activities

## Spiritual Significance

### Kaliyuga Vaikuntha

The temple is considered **Vaikuntha on Earth** during Kali Yuga because:
- Lord Venkateswara manifested to save humanity from Kali Yuga's troubles
- Darshan (viewing) of the deity brings **moksha** (liberation)
- Devotees find solace, inner peace, and divine connection

### The Cosmic Reunion

The marriage of Venkateswara and Padmavathi symbolizes:
- Reunion of divine cosmic energy
- Vishnu and Lakshmi coming together in earthly incarnations
- Eternal devotion between divine partners
- Promise of divine protection and prosperity for devotees

## Inscriptions and Heritage

The temple houses remarkable historical documentation:

- **640 inscriptions** in Kannada, Sanskrit, Tamil, and Telugu languages
- **~3000 copper plates** containing Telugu Sankirtanas of **Tallapaka Annamacharya** and his descendants
- Invaluable source for historical linguistics and musicology
- Documents centuries of devotional music tradition
