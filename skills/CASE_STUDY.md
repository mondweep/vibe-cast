## Week 2: Applications (3 Days)

### Material Preparation
**Activity:** Role-specific cover letters + recruiter emails

**For each role:**
1. Researched company (business model, recent news, culture)
2. Drafted cover letter emphasizing:
   - 20 years experience (not "decade")
   - Named clients (Lotus £150M+, Shell Energy £120M+, JLR £100M+)
   - Specific sector experience (finance, telecom, automotive)
   - "Architect + executor" positioning
   - Public projects (Content Discovery, Vibe Cast)
3. Drafted recruiter email:
   - Personal subject line (role + company + key stat)
   - 3 bullet points (why fit)
   - Call to action (ask for conversation)

**Example (PwC):**

**Cover Letter (Excerpt):**
> "I am writing to express my strong interest in the Director of AI Engineering position within PwC's Tech Catalyst UK practice. I've previously collaborated with PwC colleagues on large-scale digital transformations, and I'm excited about the opportunity to join the firm and help drive your clients' AI-enabled modernisation at scale.
> 
> My background is precisely what Tech Catalyst needs: 20 years of enterprise transformation leadership combined with deep hands-on AI engineering capability. I've architected and contributed to 40+ AI projects and systems across financial services (HSBC, Barclays, Zafin, MUFG, SEB, Lloyds), telecommunications (Shell Energy, TalkTalk), and automotive (JLR, Lotus)..."

**Recruiter Email (Excerpt):**
> **Subject:** Director AI Engineering @ PwC Tech Catalyst UK - 20 Years, £500M+ Impact
> 
> Hi PwC Recruitment Team,
> 
> I'm Mondweep Chakravorty, Head of AI Strategy & Delivery at DxSure. I saw your Director of AI Engineering opening (Tech Catalyst UK) and wanted to reach out. I've previously collaborated with PwC colleagues on enterprise transformations, and this role is compelling.
> 
> **Why I'm a strong fit:**
> - 20 years enterprise transformation + consulting delivery (Wipro Digital background)
> - 40+ AI systems; £500M+ revenue impact across finance, telecom, automotive
> - Consulting discipline: client stakeholder management, programme delivery at scale
> - AI governance + responsible deployment expertise
> - Currently mentoring 50+ professionals in agentic AI; co-founding Agentics Foundation

### Submission
- All 8 roles approved by user

- Submissions logged in tracker with dates
- Follow-up reminders set (2-week, 4-week)

**Outputs:**
- ✅ 8 applications submitted

## Key Takeaways

### What Worked

✅ **Positioning First** – Executive summary set the tone for all materials  
✅ **Role-Specific Materials** – Cover letters + recruiter emails customized per role  
✅ **Quantified Impact** – "£500M+ revenue" and "40+ systems" resonated  
✅ **Public Projects** – Content Discovery (live project) = credibility signal  
✅ **Governance Angle** – Agentics Foundation + responsible AI = CIO trust  
✅ **Website + LinkedIn Alignment** – Consistent branding across channels  
✅ **User Approval** – Never sent unapproved applications (reputation protected)

### What Could Improve

⚠️ **More Company Research** – Deep dive on each company (culture, recent news)  
⚠️ **Earlier Website** – Deployed it mid-week; could have done day 1  
⚠️ **LinkedIn Engagement** – More active engagement pre-search (relationship building)  
⚠️ **Testimonial Outreach** – Could have secured more recent testimonials

3. **Use the templates** – CV, positioning docs, cover letter, recruiter email

---

## Lessons Learned

1. **Positioning is everything** – Wrong positioning = wrong roles = wasted time
2. **Quality over quantity** – 8 curated roles > 50 generic applications
3. **User control matters** – Never automate applications without approval
4. **Public work is proof** – Content Discovery (hackathon project) = instant credibility
5. **Follow-ups work** – Most callbacks come from 2-week and 4-week follow-ups
6. **Buyer focus wins** – "For CIOs navigating..." > "I'm a strategic technology leader"
7. **Track metrics** – You can't improve what you don't measure
