Grand Piano

A mobile-first piano with 7 octaves, multi-touch chords, and 5 voices.

Playing Notes

  • Tap any key to play a note. The note name appears above the keyboard.
  • Hold a key for a sustained tone; lift your finger to stop.
  • Slide your finger across keys for a glissando sweep.
  • Multi-touch — press several keys at once with multiple fingers to play chords directly on the keyboard.

Chord Strip

  • The row of buttons above the keyboard provides one-tap chords.
  • Tap a chord button for a quick strum. Hold (200 ms+) to sustain the chord; release to stop.
  • Swipe left/right on the strip to scroll through all chords: major, minor, 7th, M7, m7 for every root note.
  • Chords automatically update to match the octave visible on the keyboard.

Scrolling the Keyboard

  • The full piano spans 7 octaves (C1 – B7). Swipe left/right on the keys to scroll freely.
  • Use the ◀ ▶ arrow buttons on either side of the keyboard to scroll. Hold an arrow to keep scrolling.
  • The Octave ◀ 4 ▶ control in the top bar jumps directly to a specific octave.
  • The octave number and chord strip update automatically as you scroll.

Sustain Pedal

  • Press and hold the SUSTAIN bar at the bottom. While held, any notes you play will keep ringing after you lift your finger off the key.
  • Release the pedal to fade out all sustained notes.
  • On a desktop keyboard, hold the Spacebar for sustain.

Sound Voices

  • Use the Sound dropdown to switch between five timbres:
  • Grand — rich harmonics with natural decay (default)
  • Bright — sharp, present tone
  • Warm — soft, mellow sine-wave sound
  • Electric — punchy square-wave character
  • Organ — sustained, layered harmonics

Other Controls

  • ABC — toggle note labels on/off on every key.
  • ? — open this help screen.
