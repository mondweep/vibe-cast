# Phase 3: Network & Community
## Weeks 3–6 | Building the DeFi-native network that TradFi experience can't substitute

**Goal:** Build the DeFi-native network that your institutional BD background cannot substitute. Target: 3 meaningful conversations with DeFi treasury managers or DAO contributors by end of Week 6.

**Why this matters for the job:**
You've mastered protocols (Phase 1) and can identify capital (Phase 2). Now you need to actually *talk to people*. In TradFi BD, relationships were built over golf and dinners. In DeFi, they're built in Discord, governance forums, and Twitter. You need to learn to operate in these spaces as a peer, not an outsider.

**The core skill:** You need 3 real conversations by Week 6 that lead to something. Not spam outreach. Real dialogue where someone from a DAO or institutional treasury is talking to you because they see you as knowledgeable.

## Weeks 3–4: Governance Participation

### Task 5a: Create Accounts & Read 10 Active Proposals
**What:** Create accounts on Aave, Uniswap, and Compound governance forums. Read the last 10 active proposals on each.

**Why:** Governance forums are where the real decision-makers hang out. DAOs announce things there first. Institutional allocators monitor governance carefully. If you're not reading governance, you're not in the room.

**How to execute:**
1. Create accounts:
   - https://governance.aave.com (Aave)
   - https://gov.uniswap.org (Uniswap)
   - https://forum.compound.finance (Compound)
2. Read the 10 most recent active proposals on each (30 total)
3. For each proposal, document:
   - **Title:** What's being proposed?
   - **Status:** Passed, Pending, Defeated?
   - **Why it matters:** Who benefits? What's the incentive?
   - **The debate:** What are the 2–3 main objections?
   - **Your take:** Do you think it's good? Why?
4. Create a Notion or Google Doc with all 30
5. Start noticing *patterns:*
   - Which types of proposals pass? (incentive increases? risk parameter changes?)
   - Which contributors are vocal? (Who wins debates?)
   - What do treasurers care about? (Sustainability? Capital efficiency?)

**Output for portfolio:** Governance forum analysis showing you understand protocol priorities

---

### Task 5b: Post One Substantive Comment on a Proposal
**What:** Post one thoughtful comment on an Aave or Uniswap governance proposal. Frame it around capital efficiency or LP incentive design.

**Why:** Lurking makes you invisible. Posting makes you a participant. When someone from that DAO is looking at the proposal, they see your name. This is your first entry into the community.

**How to execute:**
1. Choose a governance proposal you have an informed opinion on
2. Read all existing comments (understand the conversation)
3. Write a 200–300 word comment that:
   - Acknowledges what others have said
   - Adds a new perspective based on *your Phase 1 & 2 experience*
   - Is specific (not generic "great proposal!")
   - Example:
     ```
     "Strong proposal. I want to add one perspective: in my experience
     profiling institutional LPs, the X factor for capital inflow isn't just
     APY, but the *perceived stability* of that yield.
     
     This proposal increases incentive spend by 20%, which grows APY short-term.
     But institutions are worried: is this sustainable?
     
     Suggestion: couple this with a transparency dashboard showing reserve
     growth and sustainability metrics. That signals confidence and would
     attract larger deposits.
     
     I built a Dune dashboard tracking institutional capital flows [link]
     and institutions explicitly asked for sustainability proof."
     ```
4. Post it
5. Monitor replies (you might get messages from DAO members)

**Output for portfolio:** Public governance forum comment (proof you participate)

## Weeks 5–6: Outreach & Events

### Task 6a: Identify 10 DeFi Treasury Managers & Send Tailored Outreach
**What:** Identify 10 DAO/protocol treasury managers whose governance activity you've followed. Send tailored outreach on LinkedIn or X — lead with a specific insight about their strategy.

**Why:** This is the jump from "community member" to "BD professional." You're no longer anonymous. You're reaching out with credibility because you've done homework and can speak their language.

**How to execute:**
1. Identify 10 treasury managers:
   - From your LP target list (Phase 2)
   - From Discord conversations
   - From governance forums (look for frequent commenters on treasury topics)
2. For each, do homework:
   - Read their last 5 Twitter posts about treasury strategy
   - Check their recent governance votes (what do they support?)
   - Find a specific insight: "You just voted for X, which suggests you care about Y"
3. Craft tailored outreach (not templated):
   ```
   Subject: Thought on [Treasury/DAO Name]'s yield strategy
   
   Hi [Name],
   
   I've been analyzing institutional capital flows into DeFi over the past month
   (Dune dashboard here: [link]). I noticed [Treasury] increased stablecoin
   positions by [X]% while reducing [Asset] exposure.
   
   That move mirrors what I've seen other treasuries do before they move capital
   into [Protocol]. It suggests you're hunting for [yield type].
   
   I'd love to share some of my research on [Topic] — might be useful for your
   next treasury allocation decision.
   
   Would you have 15 mins next week?
   
   Best,
   [Your name]
   ```
4. Key elements:
   - Proof you've done homework (reference specific action)
   - Your credibility (Dune dashboard, research)
   - Why they should talk to you (you've found patterns they'd find useful)
   - Low-friction ask (15 mins, not a huge time commitment)
5. Send to 10 people, expect 20–30% response rate
6. **Goal:** 3 conversations scheduled by Week 6

**Output for portfolio:** Outreach email templates + response rate data (shows you can do BD)

### Task 6b: Attend One DeFi-Focused Event
**What:** Attend one DeFi event in person or online (ETHGlobal, DeFi conference, London Web3 meetup).

**Why:** Network effects are real. Meeting people in person (or in an event video call) is 10x more powerful than Discord. You need to show up and be present.

**How to execute:**
1. Find an event:
   - https://ethglobal.com (ETHGlobal next event)
   - "DeFi conference" + your location
   - Local Web3 meetups (search on meetup.com or LinkedIn)
2. Attend:
   - Take notes on presentations (you'll reference them)
   - Go to social hours (this is where real networking happens)
   - Talk to people:
     - "What's your focus?" (Listen more than you talk)
     - "I'm learning about institutional capital in DeFi — what challenges do you see?"
     - "What's the biggest thing your DAO/fund is working on right now?"
3. Collect:
   - Names and Twitter handles
   - What they're working on
   - One interesting thing each person said
4. Follow up:
   - The next day, DM them on Twitter: "Great chat at [event]. I meant to ask you about [specific thing]. Let me know if you want to continue the conversation."
   - 50% will respond

**Output for portfolio:** Event photos + follow-up conversations + connections made (shows you network)

### Task 6c: Research Institutional Custody Providers
**What:** Study Fireblocks, Copper, and Anchorage — major institutional DeFi custody providers. Understand their LP onboarding flows.

**Why:** Institutions don't just put capital in protocols. They use custody providers for security. Understanding the custody layer means understanding the *institutional customer journey*. If you know Fireblocks' process, you can speak to treasury managers about their constraints.

**How to execute:**
1. Visit:
   - https://www.fireblocks.com
   - https://www.copper.co
   - https://www.anchorage.com
2. For each, document:
   - What do they do? (custody, settlement, automation?)
   - How does a treasury manager use them?
   - What DeFi protocols can they interact with?
   - What are the friction points? (KYC, documentation, delays?)
3. Read:
   - Their case studies (how are real institutions using them?)
   - Blog posts about DeFi integration
   - Community discussions (Discord, forums)
4. Write a 300-word summary:
   ```
   "Institutional LP Onboarding Workflow:
   
   Treasury manager wants to deploy £5M to Aave.
   They use [custody provider] for security.
   The custody provider integrates with Aave via [method].
   This creates [constraint]: e.g., no flash loans, 24-hour settlement, etc.
   
   If a protocol understands these constraints and offers [solution],
   it wins the institutional LP."
   ```

**Output for portfolio:** Institutional custody workflow analysis

### Task 6d: Leverage Your TradFi Network
**What:** Identify 5 contacts from HSBC, MUFG, Shell Energy now at crypto-adjacent institutions. Reconnect for intelligence on their DeFi allocation strategy.

**Why:** Your TradFi background is your differentiator. You have relationships with people transitioning to crypto. These people often control capital. Reconnecting is legitimate and valuable.

**How to execute:**
1. Go through your LinkedIn/email contacts
2. Identify 5 people who:
   - Worked with you at a traditional institution
   - Are now at a crypto fund, DeFi protocol, or custody provider
3. Reach out:
   ```
   Subject: Catching up + DeFi questions
   
   Hi [Name],
   
   It's been [time] since we worked together at [company]. I saw you moved
   to [crypto company] — that's awesome.
   
   I've been diving deep into DeFi over the past two months and would love
   to hear about your journey. How did you make the transition? What's [company]'s
   focus in DeFi?
   
   Also curious: from your perspective, what barriers do traditional institutions
   still have around DeFi allocation?
   
   Would love to catch up. Coffee call next week?
   ```
4. These calls are goldmines for:
   - Intel on why institutions hesitate
   - Introductions to their network
   - Understanding institutional decision-making
5. After each call, document:
   - Key insights about institutional hesitation
   - People they mention that might be good to know
   - What they're working on

**Output for portfolio:** TradFi contact reconnection insights + referral network
